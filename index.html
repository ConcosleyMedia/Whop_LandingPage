Inside the Kill Filter

Most AI tells you yes. The Kill Filter is built to say no.

Ask Claude or ChatGPT if your idea is good — you'll get a paragraph that says yes. AI is trained to be encouraging. That's why your last 12 ideas all sounded great in the chat and zero of them shipped.

The Kill Filter is calibrated the other way. It scores every idea against five criteria pulled from market data — not vibes — and it's biased toward kill. The ones that survive are the ones worth your next 30 days.

  • Paying proximity How close is the buyer to a credit card right now?
  • Build scope Can you ship a working MVP in 30 days?
  • Validation cost Can you test demand for under $50?
  • Unfair advantage Why you, and not someone faster or cheaper?
  • Retention shape Would they come back next month, or use it once?
Output:a score out of 50, a verdict, and either the four build files (CLAUDE.md, spec.md, stack.md, cut-list.md) or a one-line reason it’s dead.
