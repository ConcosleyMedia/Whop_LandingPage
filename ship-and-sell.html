If you're reading this, I probably sent you the link. This page isn't public. It's a memo for the handful of people where group coaching is the wrong fit.

Why this exists

Most people who want to ship a product belong in Build Room ($9/mo) or the Ship Loop cohort ($297/mo). Those formats work — small room, weekly rhythm, peers doing the same thing. For a small minority, the cohort format is wrong. Usually because you're further along than most cohort members, or you're moving faster than a group rhythm allows, or you want someone who'll actually sit with your code and pair through hard parts.

If any of that is you, this is the format.

How it works

Four weeks. 1:1 with me. No group.

Week 01
Idea & validation We audit what you're working on, cut the wrong parts, run the 72-hour validation, and lock the scope.
Week 02
Blueprint & stack MVP scoped in one session. Stack decided, schema sketched, subagent roster built. GitHub and deployment set up the right way.
Week 03
Build You build with Claude Code. Two 60-min 1:1 calls. Daily async in our shared channel. I pair through the walls — Supabase, Stripe, auth, deploy.
Week 04
Launch & first users Waitlist, outreach, first paid ad test. We go from deployed URL to first 10 paying users. You end week 4 with a live, working product and real customers.

The only promise I make

You ship in 30 days, or I work with you for another 30 free. No refund hand-wringing, no exit interview. The program is built on an outcome, not on time spent.

If you don't show up to calls or open Claude Code, I can't help. The guarantee assumes you do the work.

Who this is for

  • Build Room members who've outgrown the $9 format
  • Operators at day jobs who want to ship something on the side but can't wait for a cohort
  • Founders who've built something in Lovable/Bolt and need to own the code before launching
  • People who've bought ShipFast, a Skool program, or 3+ courses and still haven't shipped

Not for you if: you want someone to build your idea for you, you're not willing to look at code, or your idea needs a real engineering team. Mention it when you join the waitlist and I'll point you somewhere better.
